Tester Reflection:

Being a good tester taught me that a good wireframe is predictable. While new features and fun animations can be very compelling, having a strong foundation of clear iconography and text placement makes the most accessible application. Implementing actual text where appropriate gives less wiggle room for the user to guess where to click or what to do. Not being able to click on different options left me guessing a few times where it could have been beneficial for prototyping to be involved. Also, as a tester I was able to see different realizations of the same prompt I am undertaking which helps me learn from my peers and use this newfound knowledge to further my design. Overall, being a tester helped reinforce the idea that following convention isn’t bad when it comes to building the structure of an application and further differentiation can be done in higher fidelity iterations.
