Interview Notes 04/03/2024

Ollie:

  • Offers replay of matches and engaging statistics like shots during the game.
  • Pain Point:
  • Lack of parental attention to children's game news; despite sending game invites, parents often don't respond, possibly due to communication issues.

HEJA:

  • Communication App: utilized for team communication, but limited to a single team, raising concerns for parents with multiple children.

TeamSnap/Sports Engine

  • These platforms help in scheduling, team balancing, and auto-scheduling, albeit with some limitations.

Injury Management:

  • Current apps aren't effectively utilized for injury management; coaches need a streamlined way to update parents and administration about injuries.
  • Desired features include injury alerts and emotional support for the team, akin to a familial bond.
  • INJURIES HAVE LIABILITY AND PRIVACY CONCERNS SO PROBABLY STAY AWAY

Daily Routine (Coach):

  • Work involves coaching 4th graders at the YMCA
  • Adapting plans based on game and practice dynamics, focusing on drills and calisthenics.
  • Lack of structured practice plans, relying more on memory and intuition for player progress.
  • Desire for improved communication tools, especially for managing notifications and game details.

Game Preparation:

  • Challenges include player rotation management and tracking player stats on the field.
  • Considerations for warm-ups, team structure, and opponent analysis.

App ideas:

  • Depends on what audience catering to
  • Youth
  • High school / Collegiate
  • Some QOL changes
  • Parental profile multiple kiddos
  • Notification capability
  • Etc.
  • Planning capability for coach during or before the games
