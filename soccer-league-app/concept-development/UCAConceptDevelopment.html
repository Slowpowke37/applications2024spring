  1. The primary problem the app is intended to address.
  1. This app is intended to improve quality of life features commonly seen in other apps as well as introducing a sponsor/scholarship component to aid in league fees. It is an all-inclusive app that allows every type of user to perform their necessary tasks in one place.

  1. A short summary of the inspiration for the idea, and key evidence (observations, insights, interview notes, etc.) supporting the concept.
  1. Finances (mostly research but also covered a bit in interviews):                        
  1. Staffing shortages
  1. Low pay, demanding schedules, lack of professional development opportunities→ large team sizes→ less individual attention and instruction
  2. Clubs should invest in development of staff and provide opportunities for professional growth, can also leverage parent volunteers and former players
  1. Financial shortages
  1. Maintaining pitches, compensating coaches and staff, purchasing necessary equipment
  2. Funded by fundraising events, membership fees, sponsorships, and donations
  3. Quality of services could decline with lack of funds
  4. Clubs should explore creative and diverse revenue streams: partnerships with local business, hosting tournaments, running community event, renting out facilities not in use
  1. Financial Challenges to join a league
  • Costs ‘“pay to play”
  • Most clubs range from $1000-$3000/yr
  • Some leagues have recreational options for less but typically for limited age ranges or through some form of modified play
  • Travel: flights and hotels $1000-$5000/yr
  • Equipment $500-$1000.yr
  • Some talent gets left out
  • Solutions: talent evaluation, sponsorship, scholarships
  1. Statistics and videos
  1. Player interview
  1. Mentioned being able to see the game live with live stats (who’s on the field, what shots are being made, what passes are being made, time scored, who’s in possession, etc) when he couldn’t make games was nice
  2. Towards the end of the season, an MVP is chosen, and having stats is really helpful in deciding who gets it
  3. Players (especially as they get older) really start to care about their own stats and keeping track of their progress
  1. Coach interview:
  1. Not super integral at lower levels
  1. More situational and game specific changes
  1. Could be a breakout market, feels like there is a desire for stats but people don’t really know what to keep track of as opposed to other sports like football, basketball, and volleyball.
  1. Communication
  1. Player interview
  1. Communication is important in knowing details such as practice/game cancellations, departure times/locations (and changes), contacting people for extra uniforms, get-togethers, etc
  2. Says he doesn’t use soccer league apps often except for just regular chat functions and important updates [a]
  3. Mentioned how an alarm/reminder feature would be useful
  1. Sometimes game start times or departure times change, and it can be especially inconvenient if it is moved up and the player ends up running late – having a feature set to ex: “Remind me 1 hour before departure” and automatically update that changed departure time or “New Departure Time: X:XX”, it can be useful in knowing the correct time to leave
  1. Parent interview
  1. Uses app(s) to communicate their kid’s availability, volunteer
  2. Mentioned that because she is disconnected from the school socially, had difficulties finding out important changes/updates, or even basic information about the game like where it was and how to get there
  1. Coach interview:
  1. Difficulty communicating with parents mainly
  1. They will care as much as they want to care
  1. Makes it difficult to plan for games and practices around consistent attendance
  1. Mostly an issue for youth leagues
  1. Notification spam leads some parents to turn them off
  1. Solution: don’t send notifications for each individual profile affected but only to notify the overall account
  1. Example: instead of sending 3 notifications for a parent that has 3 players on 3 different teams for a storm warning; send the parent one notification due to the decision being league-wide
  1. Admin interview
  1. Can get confusing
  2. Overuse of platform for unnecessary communication causing people to tune out
  3. Everyone wants different type of communication
